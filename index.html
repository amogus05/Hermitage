Hermitage

The history of the Hermitage

The Hermitage began to be built with the Winter Palace, intended for Empress Elizabeth Petrovna.

It is based on the projectof the famous architect Rastrelli.

However, Catherine the Great, who ascended to the throne, became the rightful owner ofthe building a few years later.

She also began to collect works of art in the palace.

The year 1764, when the Empressacquired the first large collection of paintings,

is considered the time of the foundation of the museum

A couple of facts about the Hermitage

  1. The museum complex consists of 350 halls. To walk through them all, you will have to cover 24 kilometers.
  2. To view all the exhibits of the Hermitage, spending only 1 minute for each, it will take about 11 years of life if you devote 8 hours a day to this activity.
  3. There are about 3 million works of art and monuments of world culture in the halls and vaults of the Hermitage.
  4. The Hermitage is located in the building of the Winter Palace on the banks of the Neva River.
  5. The name of the museum complex comes from the French word meaning "place of solitude".
